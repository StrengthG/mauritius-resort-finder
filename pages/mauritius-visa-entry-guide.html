Mauritius Visa & Entry Requirements 2026

Everything you need to know before you travel — visa rules by nationality, documents required on arrival, and how to extend your stay.

Do You Need a Visa for Mauritius?

Short answer: almost certainly not.

Mauritius grants visa-free entry to citizens of over 100 countries, including the UK, all EU member states, the US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and most Commonwealth nations. No advance application is required — you receive an entry stamp at the airport on arrival.

Mauritius has one of the most open entry policies of any island nation in the Indian Ocean. The government has deliberately maintained visa-free access for tourism because the industry accounts for a significant share of GDP. If you hold a passport from a developed economy, the entry process is straightforward: arrive, present your documents at immigration, receive your 60-day stamp, and you're in.

The handful of nationalities that do require a visa in advance should contact the Mauritius High Commission or Embassy in their country of residence before travelling. Mauritius does not currently offer a pre-travel e-visa for tourist visits.

Documents Required on Arrival

Immigration at Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (SSR) is efficient. The process typically takes 10–25 minutes after landing. Have these four items ready before you reach the desk.

🛂
Valid passport

Must be valid for the duration of your stay. Mauritius does not legally require 6 months of remaining validity beyond your departure date, but airlines and immigration officers may turn you back if validity is tight. A 6-month buffer is strongly recommended.

✈️
Return or onward ticket

You must be able to show you have a confirmed departure from Mauritius. A return flight booking is sufficient. Officers may ask to see it at the desk — have it accessible on your phone or printed.

🏨
Proof of accommodation

A hotel booking confirmation for at least the first night of your stay. If staying with friends or family, a letter of invitation with their Mauritius address and ID details is accepted.

💳
Sufficient funds

Mauritius does not specify a minimum cash amount, but immigration may ask how you intend to finance your stay. A credit card and evidence of a hotel booking that has been pre-paid are generally sufficient.

Health and vaccination requirements

As of 2026, Mauritius has no mandatory vaccination requirements for tourist entry. No COVID-19 certificates, PCR tests, or health declarations are required. Yellow fever vaccination is required only if you are arriving directly from a country listed as a yellow fever risk zone — primarily parts of Sub-Saharan Africa and South America.

Customs allowances

Duty-free allowances on arrival: up to 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco; 1 litre of spirits and 2 litres of wine or beer; up to 100ml of perfume or 500ml of eau de toilette. Items above these allowances may be subject to customs duty. Currency import is unrestricted for amounts up to MUR 500,000 (approximately USD 10,000 equivalent); larger sums must be declared.

Arriving at SSR International Airport

Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (IATA code: MRU) is located at Plaisance in the south-east of the island — about 45 minutes from Port Louis and 60–90 minutes from the north coast resort areas of Grand Baie and Balaclava.

Immigration process

Follow signs for "Arrivals" and join the immigration queue. Queues are separated by nationality at peak times (direct flights from London, Paris, and Dubai tend to arrive in the early morning). Average waiting time is 10–20 minutes. Present your passport, return ticket, and hotel confirmation. The officer will stamp your passport and note the permitted duration of stay. Ask if you'd like to confirm the exact date your permission expires.

Extending Your Stay Beyond 60 Days

If you want to stay in Mauritius longer than your initial 60-day stamp allows, you must apply for an extension at the Passport and Immigration Office (PIO) in Port Louis before your current permission expires.

How to apply

  1. Visit the PIO at Line Barracks, Abercrombie Street, Port Louis (open Monday–Friday, 09:00–15:30).
  2. Bring your passport (with at least one blank page), your return or onward ticket, proof of accommodation for the extended period, and evidence of funds to support your stay.
  3. Complete the extension form at the counter. Officers process applications on the day; expect 1–3 hours.
  4. Pay the administrative fee (nominal — typically MUR 500–1,500, around USD 10–30).
  5. A new stamp will be added to your passport with the extended departure date.

Extensions are typically granted in 30- to 60-day increments. The maximum continuous stay for most tourists is 180 days within any 12-month period. Overstaying your permission is an offence — fines and deportation orders can result. Apply early; do not wait until the last day.

Practical Entry Tips

Travel insurance

Mauritius does not require travel insurance as a condition of entry, but it is strongly recommended. Medical care in Mauritius is good at private hospitals but can be expensive for uninsured visitors. A standard travel policy covering medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and lost luggage is sufficient. Note that some insurers require you to declare adventure activities (kitesurfing, diving, cliff-jumping) separately.

Currency and money on arrival

The local currency is the Mauritian Rupee (MUR). ATMs at SSR Airport are available in the arrivals hall and dispense rupees against international debit and credit cards. The exchange rate at airport ATMs is generally fair — better than currency exchange desks at the terminal. Most resort hotels also accept payment in USD, EUR, and GBP directly. Small local restaurants, market vendors, and taxis work in rupees; bringing MUR 2,000–5,000 in cash for your first day is practical.

SIM cards and connectivity

Local SIM cards (Emtel or My.T) are available at the airport on arrival for approximately MUR 300–500 (USD 6–10) including data credit. Coverage across Mauritius is excellent. If you're working remotely or rely on a constant connection, a local SIM is significantly cheaper than international roaming charges from UK or US carriers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do UK citizens need a visa for Mauritius?

No. UK citizens receive a 60-day entry stamp on arrival at SSR International Airport at no charge. No advance visa application is required. You will need a valid passport, a return or onward ticket, and proof of accommodation. The stay can be extended at the Passport and Immigration Office in Port Louis for a total stay of up to 180 days.

Do US citizens need a visa for Mauritius?

No. US citizens enter Mauritius visa-free for up to 60 days. The process is an arrival stamp at immigration — no advance application, no e-visa, no fee. Requirements are the same as for UK visitors: valid passport, return ticket, and proof of accommodation.

How long can I stay in Mauritius without a visa?

Most visitors from the UK, EU, US, Canada, Australia, and comparable nations receive 60 days on arrival. This can be extended at the Passport and Immigration Office in Port Louis before the initial stamp expires. The maximum tourist stay is generally 180 days in any 12-month period.

What documents do I need to enter Mauritius?

Four things: a valid passport (6+ months of validity recommended), a return or onward ticket, proof of accommodation for at least your first night (a hotel booking confirmation), and the ability to demonstrate sufficient funds for your stay. Travel insurance is strongly recommended but not a legal entry requirement.

Is there an e-visa for Mauritius?

No. Mauritius does not currently operate a pre-travel e-visa system for tourist visits from visa-exempt countries. For nationalities that travel visa-free, no advance registration is required. Travellers from countries that require a visa must apply through the Mauritius High Commission or Embassy in their home country before departing.

Can I extend my stay in Mauritius beyond 60 days?

Yes. Visit the Passport and Immigration Office at Line Barracks in Port Louis before your initial stamp expires. Bring your passport, return ticket, proof of accommodation for the extended period, and evidence of funds. Extensions are processed on the day and typically add 30–60 days. The maximum tourist stay is 180 days in any 12-month period.
