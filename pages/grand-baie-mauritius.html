There are three rated hotels within Grand Baie itself, spanning ultra-luxury to affordable boutique. Each sits within walking distance of the village strip, the public beach at La Cuvette, and the main watersports pier.

Our scoring places it at 9.2/10 overall, driven by exceptional service scores and the highest location score of any Grand Baie hotel. What Grand Baie Is Actually Like

The beach

La Cuvette is the main public beach — a sheltered, horseshoe-shaped cove with calm, swimmable water year-round. It's not the widest beach in Mauritius (Belle Mare on the east coast is broader and quieter), but the calm lagoon makes it one of the safest for swimming. The beach at Royal Palm and Oberoi is semi-private and slightly wider.

Within 10 minutes drive: Mon Choisy is a long, shaded public beach popular with locals at weekends; Pereybere has excellent shallow snorkelling over coral. Cap Malheureux's beach, 15 minutes east, is photogenic but less swimmable due to the rocky shoreline.

Watersports and activities

Grand Baie is the island's watersports capital. From the main pier, you can book:

  • Catamaran day trips to Île Plate and Coin de Mire — uninhabited offshore islands with exceptional snorkelling
  • Scuba diving — Cathedral Cave and Shark Point are nearby dive sites; the Balaclava Marine Park is 20 minutes west
  • Whale shark and dolphin encounters — seasonal, typically June to October
  • Deep-sea fishing — marlin, tuna, and mahi-mahi
  • Kitesurfing and windsurfing — best at Mon Choisy beach

Dining and nightlife

Grand Baie's restaurant strip along Royal Road offers genuine variety — Indian, Creole, Japanese, French, and seafood restaurants catering to both tourists and locals. Unlike hotel-only destinations, you can walk to dinner here. Sunset Boulevard and Banana Beach Club are reliable local favourites. The nightlife is relatively mild by international standards but is the liveliest on the island.

Shopping

La Croisette is a purpose-built shopping centre at the eastern edge of Grand Baie with international brands, a supermarket, and restaurants. It is the largest shopping destination outside Port Louis and is useful for picking up essentials or spending a rainy morning. Grand Baie's main road also has jewellery shops, craft markets, and textile sellers.

Grand Baie vs the East Coast

The most common comparison for first-time visitors. Here is how the two areas actually differ:

Factor Grand Baie (North) East Coast (Belle Mare / Trou d'Eau Douce)
Beach quality Good — calm, sheltered, smaller Excellent — wider, whiter, more secluded
Atmosphere Active, social, village feel Peaceful, secluded, resort-focused
Restaurants outside hotel Many — within walking distance Few — mostly hotel dining
Watersports variety Highest on the island Good, mostly hotel-based
Nightlife Moderate — bars and clubs nearby Minimal
Family suitability Good — calm lagoon, activities Good — wider beaches, calmer hotels
Honeymoon seclusion Moderate — more foot traffic High — genuinely secluded

The bottom line: choose Grand Baie if you want variety, flexibility, and activity. Choose the east coast if you want the most pristine beaches and resort seclusion. For rankings of east coast properties, see our best beach resorts guide and the Belle Mare hotel rankings.

Who Should Stay in Grand Baie

Grand Baie is the right choice if you:

  • Want to combine beach time with excursions, watersports, and dining out
  • Are travelling as a family with older children who need activities beyond the pool
  • Want flexibility to eat at different restaurants each evening without booking hotel packages
  • Are interested in diving or snorkelling — the offshore islands and marine parks are most accessible from here
  • Are visiting Mauritius for the first time and want to understand the island beyond one resort

Consider elsewhere if you:

  • Want a completely secluded honeymoon retreat (look at Paradise Cove, Cap Malheureux, or the Le Morne peninsula in the south-west)
  • Prioritise the widest, most pristine beaches (east coast wins on this)
  • Want a true all-inclusive where leaving the resort is not part of the plan

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Grand Baie known for in Mauritius?

Grand Baie is the social hub of northern Mauritius — known for its sheltered lagoon, watersports (diving, catamaran trips, whale and dolphin watching), restaurant strip, nightlife, and the La Croisette shopping centre. It is livelier and more activity-focused than the quiet east or south coasts.

What is the best hotel in Grand Baie?

Royal Palm Beachcomber Luxury scores 9.2/10 in our independent dataset — the highest-rated hotel on the north coast and one of the top five in Mauritius overall. It offers 99 suites, a private beach, and the only truly ultra-luxury experience in Grand Baie. The Oberoi Beach Resort is the closest rival at 8.8/10 with a more intimate feel.

Is Grand Baie good for families?

Yes. Grand Baie's sheltered, calm lagoon is safer for children than the open ocean beaches of the east coast. The range of watersports, boat trips to offshore islands, and the La Croisette mall give families plenty to do. Lux* Grand Gaube (10 minutes north-east) is the highest-rated family-oriented resort in the area.

Is Grand Baie worth it over the east coast?

The east coast (Belle Mare, Trou d'Eau Douce) has wider, whiter beaches and more exclusive seclusion. Grand Baie wins on activity variety, dining options, and nightlife. If you want a self-contained all-inclusive retreat, head east. If you want restaurants, day trips to islands, and a buzz, Grand Baie is the better base.

What beaches are near Grand Baie?

La Cuvette is the main public beach in Grand Baie — small but well-sheltered and clean. A 10-minute drive reaches Mon Choisy (long, quiet, ideal for swimming) and Pereybere (colourful, snorkel-friendly, shallow). Further east, Cap Malheureux is a picture-postcard fishing village with a red-roofed church that appears on every postcard of Mauritius.

When is the best time to visit Grand Baie?

May to November is the dry season with low humidity and steady south-east trade winds — ideal for watersports and diving. July to September can be cool and windy on the north coast. December to April is hot and humid with occasional cyclone risk, but hotel rates drop significantly. For the best balance of weather and value, target May–June or September–October.
