Adults-Only Resorts in Mauritius

Mauritius has two resorts that formally exclude children, and a handful more that attract almost exclusively adult guests by design. This guide covers all of them — scored on the same independent criteria we apply to every property on the island.

What "adults-only" means in Mauritius

Mauritius has no island-wide adults-only standard. The term covers two genuinely different things: resorts that contractually exclude children (formal policy), and resorts that permit children but attract very few by virtue of their pricing, character, or age minimums. This guide distinguishes between both — because if you've been burned by a "couples preferred" hotel that had a crèche in the lobby, the difference matters.

The formally adults-only properties in Mauritius are Paradise Cove Boutique Hotel (18+) and Lagoon Attitude (16+), both in Cap Malheureux on the northern coast. These are the only hotels where children are contractually excluded — if you book, you will not share the pool with a family on holiday.

The predominantly adult category includes Royal Palm Beachcomber Luxury (16+ minimum age), Constance Prince Maurice (no formal exclusion but a no-children character by design), and Sea Diamond Boutique Hotel & Spa (boutique scale and pricing that draws almost exclusively couples). These properties permit children but attract very few — they are genuinely quiet, couple-focused stays without the guarantee of a child-free environment.

For most couples travelling to Mauritius, the broader category of quiet luxury resorts delivers the experience they want. For those for whom a strict no-children policy is non-negotiable, only Paradise Cove and Lagoon Attitude qualify.

Lagoon Attitude — Adults Only

8.7
Overall Score
📍 Cap Malheureux, Northern Mauritius · Boutique

Lagoon Attitude sits close to Paradise Cove in the same northern zone and operates a 16+ adults-only policy. It's a smaller, more casual property in the best sense — boutique without the marketing pretension. The building is low-rise, the aesthetic draws on local Mauritian character (natural wood, open-air dining, informal warmth), and the atmosphere is relaxed in a way that larger five-star hotels rarely manage. The lagoon frontage is the standout asset: shallow, clear, and with natural coral visible from the beach. Service is warm and attentive without the formal precision of a larger operation. The value score (8.2/10) is among the highest in our dataset — Lagoon Attitude delivers a genuine premium experience at a price point that makes it one of the island's most compelling bookings for couples who want quiet over grandeur.

Who should choose an adults-only resort?

Adults-only resorts are not only for couples — they suit any traveller for whom a quiet, child-free environment is the priority. In practice the guest mix is predominantly honeymooners, anniversary travellers, and couples in their 30s–50s who want the guarantee of calm that family resorts cannot offer even when they try.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which resorts in Mauritius are strictly adults-only?

Two: Paradise Cove Boutique Hotel (18+ formal policy, Cap Malheureux) and Lagoon Attitude (16+ formal policy, Cap Malheureux). Both are in the north. Royal Palm enforces a 16+ minimum age. Other resorts are quiet and couple-focused but do not formally exclude children.

What is the best adults-only resort in Mauritius?

Among formally adults-only properties, Paradise Cove scores highest at 9.0/10 overall. For the broader category of quiet, couple-focused stays, Royal Palm Beachcomber Luxury (9.2/10) is the highest-scoring property in our entire dataset — it enforces a 16+ minimum age and operates at a level that delivers genuine adult tranquillity.

Is Mauritius good for an adults-only holiday?

Yes. Even resorts that permit children are typically quiet by character, and the island's top luxury properties cater primarily to couples and high-end leisure travellers. The formally adults-only properties (Paradise Cove, Lagoon Attitude) are two of the island's most consistently well-reviewed boutique hotels.

When is the best time to visit an adults-only resort in Mauritius?

May to November is the dry season and most comfortable — 22–27°C, low humidity. October and November give the best value within that window (rates ease after July–August peak, weather stays excellent). Paradise Cove and Lagoon Attitude are both in the north, which has the most consistently calm lagoon conditions year-round.

Are there adults-only all-inclusive resorts in Mauritius?

The formal adults-only properties (Paradise Cove, Lagoon Attitude) offer half-board and full-board options rather than full all-inclusive packages. Heritage Awali and some Beachcomber properties offer all-inclusive plans on a property-wide basis, but are not formally adults-only. Check the board plan options on each property's booking page for the current offer.
