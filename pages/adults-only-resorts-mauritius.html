Adults-Only Resorts in Mauritius

Two resorts in Mauritius formally exclude children. A handful more attract mostly adult guests by design. This guide covers all of them, scored the same way we score every other resort — no exceptions for a quiet poolside.

Mauritius has no island-wide adults-only standard. "Adults-only" means different things at different resorts. Below, we distinguish between formally adults-only (children are contractually excluded) and predominantly adult (children are permitted but the property's character, scale, or policy discourages families).

Formally Adults-Only

Formally Adults Only · 18+ Policy
Paradise Cove Boutique Hotel
9.0
Overall Score
📍 Cap Malheureux, Northern Mauritius · 67 rooms
Paradise Cove is the strongest adults-only resort in Mauritius — not just by policy, but by experience. At 67 rooms, the service ratio is exceptional: you will not wait for a sunlounger, for your drink, or for staff who know your name. The location at Cap Malheureux places it on the northern tip of the island, with direct access to a natural cove — sheltered, calm, and genuinely private. The reef is reachable by swim. The beach itself is not shared with neighbouring resorts.

The 18+ policy is strictly enforced. If you've been burned by "mostly adult" resorts before, this is the answer. Interiors lean into quiet luxury — dark wood, linen, understatement — rather than the marble-and-chandeliers formula common at larger properties. Spa is small but well-run. Dining is strong for the room count.

Formally Adults Only · 16+ Policy
Lagoon Attitude
8.7
Overall Score
📍 Cap Malheureux, Northern Mauritius · 56 rooms
Lagoon Attitude sits 800 metres from Paradise Cove, in the same northern zone, and operates a 16+ adults-only policy. It's a smaller, more casual property — boutique in the proper sense, not the marketing sense. The building is low-rise, the aesthetic is local (Mauritian wood, woven screens, open-air dining), and the vibe is relaxed in a way that larger five-star properties can't replicate even when they try.

The lagoon frontage is the key asset — shallow, clear, with natural coral visible from the beach. Service quality is high but less formal than Paradise Cove; staff are warm and attentive without the choreographed precision of a larger operation. Value score is strong: this is one of the best experiences-per-dollar properties on the island. If the formality of a traditional luxury resort isn't what you want, Lagoon Attitude is worth serious consideration.

Predominantly Adult — Quiet Alternatives

These resorts permit children but attract very few. Royal Palm enforces a minimum age of 16. The others attract mostly couples and business travellers by virtue of their positioning, pricing, or format.

16+ Policy · Predominantly Adult
Royal Palm Beachcomber Luxury
9.2
Overall Score
📍 Grand Baie, Northern Mauritius · 84 suites
Royal Palm enforces a 16-year minimum age and is the highest-scoring resort in our dataset. At 84 suites — all suites, no standard rooms — it operates at a level of service that filters its own guest profile. You don't come here with children under 16. You come here for the beach, the butler, and the kind of quiet that money actually buys. Grand Baie is livelier than Cap Malheureux, but the Royal Palm beach is private and the resort itself operates a contained world of its own.
