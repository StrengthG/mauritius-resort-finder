How We Score Resorts

Every score on this site comes from a defined, repeatable process. This page explains what goes into each number — what we measure, what we refuse to measure, and where the data comes from.

The Four Criteria

Each hotel is scored across four independent dimensions. The composite Overall Score is the arithmetic mean of all four. No criterion is weighted above another — a resort with a perfect beach but no spa cannot score as highly as one that performs consistently across all four.

Criterion 01
Location
Beach quality, lagoon depth and clarity, reef access, proximity to the airport, coastal direction (sunrise vs. sunset, prevailing wind exposure), and the quality of the immediate surroundings. We do not reward location based on a resort's marketing description of it — we use verifiable geographic and guest-reported data.
Criterion 02
Amenities
Spa presence and quality, pool facilities, fine dining (number of restaurants, cuisine range, quality data), watersports, kids club, butler service, and private beach exclusivity. We score presence and quality — not just the existence of a spa, but whether the spa has a genuine programme and qualified practitioners.
Criterion 03
Brand Credibility
Chain reputation and consistency, accreditation standards, staff training infrastructure, and the historical reliability of the brand's service delivery. An independent boutique that consistently over-delivers scores higher here than a brand that coasts on its name. Brand score is never purchased — we assess the brand, not the marketing budget.
Criterion 04
Value
Quality delivered relative to the average nightly rate. We benchmark each resort against its price bracket. A resort at $400/night needs to deliver differently than one at $1,200/night. Both can score highly on value if they deliver what the price implies — or score poorly if they don't. Price alone is not the measure.
Overall Score = (Location + Amenities + Brand + Value) ÷ 4
All scores are on a 0–10 scale. The composite is the unweighted mean of the four criteria scores.

Data Sources

Scores are based on aggregated guest review data, objective amenity inventories, and geographic verification. We do not use hotel PR materials, press releases, or any information provided by the hotels themselves as primary source data. If a hotel tells us it has an award-winning spa, we look at what guests who stayed there in the last 12 months actually said about it.

What counts as a source

  • Verified guest reviews with named stay dates (Expedia, TripAdvisor, Google Reviews, Booking.com)
  • Review volume — we weight confidence based on the number of reviews, not just the average score
  • Geographic data for location scoring (coast, reef proximity, airport distance)
  • Objective amenity presence (a kids club either exists or it doesn't)
  • Brand accreditation and historical performance data

What does not count as a source

  • Any information provided directly by the hotel or its PR agency
  • Award claims that are self-nominated or commercially determined
  • Marketing copy from the hotel's own website
  • Paid review placements (we flag these when we find them in source data and discard them)

What We Don't Do

We do

  • Score every hotel by the same process
  • Disclose all affiliate relationships
  • Publish scores even when they're unflattering
  • Show all 18 hotels regardless of affiliate status
  • Use rel="nofollow sponsored" on all affiliate links
  • Update scores when new data warrants it

We don't

  • Accept paid placements or sponsored rankings
  • Use hotel PR copy in descriptions
  • Inflate scores to protect affiliate revenue
  • Hide affiliate relationships
  • Display stale prices (we link to live rates)
  • Remove hotels from rankings for commercial reasons

Affiliate Links and Independence

This site earns revenue through Expedia affiliate links. When you click "Check prices →" and complete a booking, we may receive a commission from Expedia at no extra cost to you.

Six of the 18 hotels in our dataset currently have no affiliate link. They appear in rankings at their correct score position anyway. No hotel's ranking has ever been adjusted based on whether or not we have an affiliate relationship with it. The rankings are determined by the scoring process above, and the affiliate links are applied after scores are finalised.

Full disclosure detail is available on our affiliate disclosure page.

Score Updates

Scores are reviewed when significant new guest data is available — typically when a hotel accumulates enough new reviews to materially shift its satisfaction average, or when a property changes ownership, management, or undergoes major renovation. We do not update scores on a fixed schedule; we update them when the data warrants it. The last full scoring review was completed in May 2026.

Questions

If you believe a score is incorrect or you have information that would materially affect a hotel's rating, use the contact link in the site footer. We don't promise to change scores based on complaints — but we do read every submission and investigate claims that come with verifiable evidence.
