Mauritius Honeymoon Guide

Everything you need to plan a perfect honeymoon in Mauritius — the best resorts, ideal timing, top romantic experiences, and how to choose the right coast.

Why Mauritius for a Honeymoon?

Mauritius sits in a category of its own among Indian Ocean honeymoon destinations. Unlike the Maldives — beautiful but spatially limiting — Mauritius gives you an entire island to explore: volcanic peaks, rainforest nature reserves, Creole markets, and more than 300 km of coastline. The resort infrastructure is world-class, the dining scene is genuinely excellent, and the Mauritian warmth toward visitors is not a cliché. Most honeymooners leave wondering why they didn't book longer.

The island punches above its weight on value too. For what you pay at a comparable Maldivian water villa, Mauritius offers a larger room, more varied dining, more to do off-property, and the option to day-trip to a different coastline. That variety is exactly what makes a two-week honeymoon feel rich rather than repetitive.

Best Time to Visit Mauritius for a Honeymoon

May to October is the prime window. Temperatures hold at 22–26°C, humidity drops to comfortable levels, and the trade winds keep things fresh without being disruptive. Rain is infrequent and usually short-lived. The sea across most of the island is calm and clear — ideal for snorkelling, kayaking, and sunset cruises.

September and October are the sweet spot: excellent weather, lower prices than July–August peak, and far fewer tourists. Hotel availability is better, and the service-to-guest ratio at even busy properties feels more generous. If your honeymoon dates are flexible, aim here.

December is a popular choice for festive-season weddings and honeymoons. The island is lush, warm (28–30°C), and celebratory in atmosphere. The trade-off is higher prices and the beginning of cyclone season — travel insurance is essential.

Which Coast to Choose

Mauritius has four distinct coastlines, each with a different character. Choosing the right one — or combining two — makes a meaningful difference to the honeymoon experience.

Best Overall
North — Grand Baie & Cap Malheureux
The calmest, most sheltered lagoons year-round. Widest choice of luxury resorts. Easy access to boat trips, Flat Island, and the best snorkelling. Best: April–October.
Most Romantic
South-West — Le Morne & Bel Ombre
Dramatic basalt peninsula, kite-surfing, and the most secluded resort properties on the island. Fewer tourists, wilder landscape, and extraordinary sunsets. Best: May–October.
East — Belle Mare & Beau Champ
The island's finest white-sand beaches and two of its best luxury hotels. Quieter, more private atmosphere. Best: November–April when the lagoon is at its calmest.
West — Flic en Flac & Tamarin
Popular with locals and surfers. Good value, excellent sunsets, and reliable calm water May–October. The resort choice is less premium than north or south-west, but the character is authentic.
Split-stay idea

A popular honeymoon itinerary: 5–6 nights at a north coast resort for activities and socialising, then transfer to a south-west property (Le Morne or Bel Ombre) for a more private, secluded final stretch. The contrast is striking and you get the best of both worlds.

Romantic Experiences Not to Miss

🚢
Sunset catamaran cruise
Most resorts arrange private or semi-private charters. The north-west lagoon at sunset — flat water, pink sky, dolphin sightings — is reliably spectacular.
🌺
In-villa spa treatment
Many five-star properties bring the spa to your room or villa terrace. An hour of couples' massage with the lagoon in view is hard to improve on.
🍴
Private beach dinner
Virtually every luxury resort will arrange a candlelit dinner on the beach. Book on arrival — dates fill quickly in peak season, especially around full moon.
🐳
Swim with dolphins
Wild spinner dolphins gather in the Tamarin Bay area most mornings. Morning excursions from south-west resorts offer reliable encounters, usually before 9 am.
🏝
Private islet picnic
Several resorts (particularly on the north coast) can arrange a private boat transfer to a deserted islet with a prepared lunch basket. A half-day completely alone in paradise.
🌅
Hot air balloon at sunrise
Ballooning over the central plateau at sunrise offers a view of Mauritius that very few visitors ever see. Bookings fill weeks in advance — arrange before you arrive.

Honeymoon Packages: What's Actually Included

Most luxury resorts market a "honeymoon package," but what's included varies more than you'd expect. Here's what to look for — and what to negotiate:

  • Room decoration: Flowers, petals, fruit basket, and a welcome note on arrival. Standard at all five-star properties — confirm it's included, not charged separately.
  • One romantic dinner: Usually one complimentary candlelit dinner per stay, often on the beach or at the signature restaurant. Check if it's a fixed menu or à la carte with a set credit.
  • Couples' spa treatment: One 60–90 minute couples' massage. Higher-tier packages include two or more treatments. Book times in advance — morning slots book out fastest.
  • Boat excursion or sunset cruise: Included in some packages; charged as an add-on at others. Often the best negotiating point when booking through a travel agent.
  • Breakfast daily: Most honeymoon packages are half-board or bed-and-breakfast at minimum. Check whether your package requires a mandatory half-board supplement.
Booking tip

Booking through a specialist Mauritius travel agent often unlocks perks that the hotel's own website won't show: room upgrades, guaranteed beach-view rooms, additional spa credits, and flexible check-in. If the direct price is the same, the agent package is nearly always the better deal.

Practical Planning: Flights, Visas & Getting Around

Flights

Mauritius is served by Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (MRU), located in the south-east of the island. Direct flights from London Heathrow operate on Air Mauritius and British Airways — journey time is approximately 12 hours. From continental Europe, direct routes operate from Paris, Frankfurt, and Amsterdam. From Australia, most routes connect through Dubai or Singapore.

Booking tip: Fly into Mauritius and out via a short connecting flight to the Maldives or Seychelles for a back-to-back Indian Ocean honeymoon. Air Mauritius operates connections, and this is a popular "two-island" itinerary for honeymooners from the UK and Europe.

Visas

Most nationalities — including UK, EU, US, Australian, and Canadian citizens — receive a free visa on arrival, valid for 60–90 days. No advance visa application is required. Check the current entry requirements for your passport before travel, as rules can change.

Getting Around

Mauritius is compact (65 km north to south, 45 km east to west) and straightforward to navigate by car. Hiring a car for one or two days of self-drive exploration is highly recommended — it's the best way to see the central highlands, Chamarel's coloured earth, and the Black River Gorges National Park. Most five-star resorts also arrange private driver transfers, which removes the stress of navigating unfamiliar roads on arrival day.

If you're staying at a single resort throughout your honeymoon, a car is less essential — most luxury properties run complimentary shuttle services to nearby beaches and towns.

How to Book Your Mauritius Honeymoon

The best Mauritius honeymoon experiences are put together by specialists who know the island well enough to match you to the right property and negotiate genuinely useful extras — not just a welcome note and a fruit basket. The six weeks before and after the July–August European school holiday peak are when the best value-for-money dates cluster: the weather is still excellent, resort availability is better, and prices are meaningfully lower.

Mauritius Honeymoon — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mauritius a good honeymoon destination?
Mauritius is one of the world's top honeymoon destinations. It combines spectacular Indian Ocean beaches, world-class luxury resorts, warm and stable weather, and a private, unhurried atmosphere. The island is compact enough to explore easily, yet varied enough to offer everything from secluded lagoons to lush mountain hiking. It consistently ranks alongside the Maldives and Bora Bora as a benchmark honeymoon location.
What is the best time to visit Mauritius for a honeymoon?
May to October is ideal — dry, comfortably warm (22–26°C), and calm across most coastlines. September and October offer the best combination of excellent weather and lower prices. December is festive and popular but marks the start of cyclone season — travel insurance is essential.
How much does a Mauritius honeymoon cost?
A luxury five-star honeymoon in Mauritius typically costs £4,000–£10,000 per couple for 10 nights including flights from the UK. Shoulder months (May–June, September–October) can be 20–35% cheaper than July–August peak, with no meaningful compromise on weather quality.
Which coast of Mauritius is best for a honeymoon?
The north (Grand Baie, Cap Malheureux) and south-west (Le Morne, Bel Ombre) coasts are the top choices for honeymooners. The north offers the calmest lagoons year-round and the widest resort selection. The south-west is more dramatic and secluded, with fewer tourists and stunning sunset views.
Do Mauritius resorts offer honeymoon packages?
Yes — virtually all five-star resorts in Mauritius offer honeymoon packages typically including room decoration, a couples' spa treatment, one romantic dinner, and sometimes a sunset cruise or boat excursion. Inclusions vary significantly between properties; always confirm exactly what is included before booking.
How many nights should you spend in Mauritius on a honeymoon?
Most honeymooners spend 10–14 nights. A minimum of 7 nights is recommended — enough to settle in, explore, and not feel rushed after a long-haul flight. With 10–14 nights you can comfortably split between two coastal areas for added variety.
