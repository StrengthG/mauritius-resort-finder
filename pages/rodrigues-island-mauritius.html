How to Get from Mauritius to Rodrigues

The only practical way to reach Rodrigues from Mauritius is by air. Air Mauritius (MK) operates daily direct flights from Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (SSR, near Mahebourg) to Sir Gaëtan Duval Airport (RRG) on Rodrigues. The flight takes approximately 1 hour 20 minutes on an ATR 72 turboprop aircraft — a short hop, but the approach over the lagoon is genuinely spectacular.

Booking directly with Air Mauritius's website typically gives the best fares. Prices vary considerably: book 6–8 weeks out and expect to pay MUR 6,000–10,000 one way (around $130–$220 USD). Last-minute fares, particularly around Mauritian school holidays, can reach MUR 15,000+ each way. There is no scheduled passenger ferry service between the two islands.

A cargo vessel makes the crossing but does not accept passengers on a scheduled basis. Private charter is theoretically possible but extremely expensive. Flying is the only realistic option.

Tip: If you land at SSR in the morning after a long-haul flight, same-day connections to Rodrigues are possible — but tight. Plan at least one overnight in Mauritius before flying on, especially if your main flight arrives in the afternoon or evening.

Top 6 Things to Do in Rodrigues

Rodrigues rewards the traveller willing to slow down. There are no golf courses, no casino, and no international hotel brands — but what it lacks in infrastructure it more than compensates for with natural and cultural richness.

🪁
Kitesurfing at Anse Ally
World-class

Anse Ally, on Rodrigues's north coast, is consistently ranked among the top ten kitesurfing destinations globally. Reliable south-east trade winds from May to November produce flat, shallow lagoon conditions ideal for beginners and advanced riders alike. Several kite schools operate from the beach. Wind speeds average 20–30 knots during peak season.

🏝️
Trou d'Argent Beach
Best beach

Frequently cited as one of the Indian Ocean's most beautiful beaches, Trou d'Argent on the south-east coast is reached via a short hike through dry scrubland. Pristine white sand, crystal-clear water, and almost no other visitors. The snorkelling in the adjacent shallow lagoon is exceptional — reef fish, sea turtles, and impressive coral formations within 50 metres of shore.

🐢
François Leguat Giant Tortoise Reserve
Unique

Home to the world's largest free-roaming colony of Aldabra giant tortoises — over 15,000 individuals on 64 hectares. Tortoises roam freely; guided walks bring you within arm's reach of animals that can live 150+ years and weigh up to 300 kg. The reserve also contains a cave system and a freshwater lake. An extraordinary, humbling experience.

🦇
Caverne Patate
Natural wonder

The island's most impressive cave system, stretching several hundred metres underground. Guided tours take you past formations said to resemble the profiles of Napoleon and Josephine. Stalactites, stalagmites, and rare cave-adapted fauna in a completely dark environment. Guided access only — book in advance as group sizes are limited.

🤿
Lagoon Snorkelling & Diving
Marine reserve

Rodrigues is surrounded by one of the Indian Ocean's largest marine reserves — over 200 km² of protected reef. Water visibility regularly exceeds 30 metres. Snorkelling from Anse aux Anglais or via boat to the outer islets delivers colourful reef ecosystems largely untouched by mass tourism. Several dive operators run PADI courses and guided reef and wreck dives.

🐙
Port Mathurin Market & Culture
Local life

Port Mathurin's Saturday market is one of the most authentic in the Indian Ocean — octopus, smoked fish, local vegetables, and Rodriguan craft items sold by the island's small-scale fishing community. The octopus fishing culture is central to Rodriguan identity: at low tide, you'll see fisherwomen wading the shallows with traditional spears. A world away from resort Mauritius.

When to Visit Rodrigues

Rodrigues has a climate similar to Mauritius but is generally drier, slightly cooler in winter, and less exposed to the worst of the cyclone season. The island receives around 1,000 mm of annual rainfall — significantly less than Mauritius's 2,000 mm on the coast. The trade winds are a defining feature and drive the island's kitesurfing reputation.

Peak Season
June – November

The dry season brings consistent south-east trade winds (20–30 kt), clear blue skies, and calm seas for snorkelling. Kitesurfing conditions are at their best from July to October. Temperatures are comfortable: 22–26°C. Book accommodation and flights well in advance, as this period coincides with French and Réunionnais holidays.

Shoulder Season
March – May

The transition from wet to dry season. Showers become less frequent through April and May, and trade winds begin to build. Crowds are minimal and prices lower. A good option for travellers seeking a balance of value and decent weather. Sea conditions are still good for diving and snorkelling.

Wet Season
December – February

Warm, humid, and punctuated by heavy showers. Cyclone risk is real — Rodrigues occasionally takes direct hits from tropical storms tracking east of Mauritius. If visiting during this period, ensure travel insurance covers cyclone disruption. Some kite schools and tour operators reduce or pause operations. Not recommended for first-time visitors.

Low Season
Year-Round Fishing

Rodrigues has a significant game fishing scene — blue marlin, wahoo, and tuna are caught in the waters beyond the reef throughout the year, peaking November to April. If deep-sea fishing is your primary purpose, the warmer months give the best offshore action, though the cyclone risk is also highest.

Rodrigues vs Mauritius: What's the Difference?

Visitors often ask how Rodrigues compares to the Mauritius they've already researched. The honest answer: they are fundamentally different travel experiences. Choosing between them isn't a question of which is better — it's a question of what you want from an Indian Ocean island.

The verdict: Rodrigues suits nature lovers, kitesurfers, divers, and travellers seeking genuine off-the-beaten-track Indian Ocean culture. Mauritius suits those who want five-star resort infrastructure, diverse dining, and easy access to activities. Most travellers who've visited both recommend combining them — a few days on Rodrigues adds depth to a Mauritius luxury trip without much additional cost.

Planning a Combined Mauritius + Rodrigues Trip

A 10–14 day trip combining a Mauritius luxury resort stay with 3–5 days in Rodrigues is the most popular approach. The flight is short enough that adding Rodrigues doesn't feel like a separate trip — it's a genuinely easy extension. The main planning consideration is flight timing: Air Mauritius schedules vary, so check the MK website early to ensure your preferred Rodrigues dates are available.

Rodrigues Island — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get from Mauritius to Rodrigues Island?

Air Mauritius (MK) operates direct flights from SSR (Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam) to RRG (Sir Gaëtan Duval Airport, Rodrigues). The flight takes approximately 1 hour 20 minutes on an ATR 72 turboprop. Expect 2–5 flights per day depending on season; one-way fares range from MUR 6,000 to 15,000+ ($130–$330 USD). No scheduled passenger ferry service exists.

Is Rodrigues Island worth visiting?

Yes — for travellers seeking unspoilt nature, world-class kitesurfing, and genuine Creole culture without resort crowds. Rodrigues is not the destination for five-star hotel amenities, but it delivers pristine lagoons, 15,000+ free-roaming giant tortoises, and a pace of life impossible to find on mainland Mauritius.

What is Rodrigues Island famous for?

Rodrigues is primarily known for world-class kitesurfing at Anse Ally (consistently ranked top 10 globally), the François Leguat Giant Tortoise & Cave Reserve (15,000+ free-roaming tortoises), the Caverne Patate cave system, and one of the Indian Ocean's largest protected marine reserves with exceptional dive and snorkel visibility.

Is Rodrigues part of Mauritius?

Yes. Rodrigues is an autonomous island forming part of the Republic of Mauritius, with its own elected Regional Assembly since 2002. It uses the Mauritian Rupee and the same visa regime as mainland Mauritius. No additional passport check or documentation is needed when flying domestically between the two islands.

How long should I spend in Rodrigues?

Three to five days covers the essential highlights: François Leguat Reserve, Trou d'Argent beach, Caverne Patate, a lagoon boat trip, and Port Mathurin's Saturday market. Five days allows time for kitesurfing lessons and a more relaxed pace. Rodrigues is typically added as an extension to a longer Mauritius resort stay.

Do I need a visa for Rodrigues Island?

No. Rodrigues is legally part of the Republic of Mauritius, so any entry permission covering Mauritius also covers Rodrigues. Citizens of most countries (EU, UK, US, Canada, Australia, most Commonwealth nations) can visit visa-free for up to 90 days. The domestic flight between Mauritius and Rodrigues has no passport control.
