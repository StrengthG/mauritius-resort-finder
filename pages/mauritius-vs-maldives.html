Mauritius vs Maldives 2026 — Which Indian Ocean Island Should You Choose?

An honest, data-backed comparison of both islands across beaches, hotel quality, activities, cost, and travel style — so you choose the right one for your trip.

The Short Answer

Both the Maldives and Mauritius rank among the world's finest island destinations. They appeal to different travellers, which makes the choice less about which is "better" and more about what you actually want from a holiday.

Choose Mauritius if you want…
  • Land activities beyond the beach (hiking, golf, culture)
  • A wider range of hotel tiers and price points
  • An island you can explore independently by car
  • Real local cuisine, markets, and towns
  • Family travel with varied activities for all ages
  • The option to leave the resort and discover the island
Choose Maldives if you want…
  • Total seclusion on a private island resort
  • Overwater bungalows above a turquoise lagoon
  • World-class diving and snorkelling directly from your room
  • The "disappear from the world" experience
  • A honeymoon centred entirely on the ocean and each other
  • No reason to ever leave the resort

If you're struggling to choose, the sections below compare both islands across every meaningful dimension.

Cost Comparison: Mauritius vs Maldives

The Maldives is consistently more expensive than Mauritius at every tier — the private-island model creates captive pricing, since there are no local restaurants, supermarkets, or alternative accommodation options once you're on a resort island.

The hidden cost in the Maldives is the captive pricing: every meal, every drink, every activity is priced with no competition. In Mauritius, the option to eat locally — even once per day — can save $80–$120/day per couple. Over seven nights, that's a meaningful difference.

Activities: Where Each Island Wins

Mauritius activities the Maldives cannot match

  • Golf: Two championship courses — the Ernie Els-designed Anahita course and the Bernhard Langer-designed Île aux Cerfs Golf Club. The Maldives has one nine-hole course on Kuredu Island.
  • Hiking: Black River Gorges National Park covers 67 km² of forest, waterfalls, and endemic birdlife. The Maldives has no hills, let alone hiking trails.
  • Cultural exploration: Port Louis's Central Market, Chamarel's seven-coloured earth, the colonial heritage of Mahebourg and Curepipe, Hindu temples, mosques, and Creole villages.
  • Local food: Roulottes, dholl puri stalls, Indian vegetarian restaurants, Creole family dining. The Maldives is almost entirely resort dining.
  • Car travel: You can drive across Mauritius in 90 minutes. Renting a car and exploring independently costs $35/day. In the Maldives, you're on a sandbar — there's nowhere to drive.

Maldives experiences Mauritius cannot replicate

  • Overwater bungalows: Glass floors over the lagoon, direct ocean access from your terrace. Mauritius has almost none.
  • Underwater encounters from shore: Many Maldives resorts have house reefs directly off the beach — step in and you're snorkelling with manta rays.
  • Absolute seclusion: Being on a private island with 30–50 other guests, no roads to anywhere, total disconnection. No Mauritius resort fully replicates this.
  • Night snorkelling: Bioluminescent plankton at several Maldives resorts — a genuinely rare experience.

Can You Visit Both on the Same Trip?

Yes — and for couples who can't choose, it's a genuinely practical itinerary. Both islands route naturally through Dubai, which makes a twin-centre holiday straightforward via Emirates.

A typical dual-island trip: UK → Dubai → Mauritius (7 nights) → Dubai (1 night) → Maldives (5 nights) → UK. The Dubai stop adds one transit night but costs very little when flights are booked together and can be used as a rest day.

The downside: 13 nights minimum feels rushed at both destinations if you want to explore properly. Most couples who do both prefer 10 nights in Mauritius (enough to see the island) and 7 nights in the Maldives. That's 17 nights abroad — significant, but not unreasonable for a once-in-a-decade honeymoon.

Budget for a dual-centre from the UK: $22,000–$38,000 for two including flights, hotels, and transfers, depending on resort tier chosen at each destination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mauritius or Maldives better for a honeymoon?

Both are exceptional honeymoon destinations. The Maldives wins on seclusion and underwater romance — overwater bungalows directly above a lagoon are genuinely unmatched. Mauritius wins on variety: beaches plus mountains, culture, golf, and experiences beyond snorkelling. For pure isolation, choose Maldives. For a full holiday experience with cultural exploration alongside beach luxury, Mauritius is stronger.

Which is cheaper — Mauritius or Maldives?

Mauritius is consistently cheaper at every tier. A top-scoring Mauritius resort like One&Only Le Saint Géran starts at $940/night; a comparable Maldives property starts around $1,400/night. The gap widens significantly at the mid-range: Mauritius has independently scored hotels from $190/night; the Maldives has very few genuine mid-range options. Off-resort dining in Mauritius can save $80–$120/day per couple — impossible in the Maldives where you're captive to resort pricing.

Does Mauritius have overwater bungalows?

Mauritius has very few overwater bungalows — the lagoon topography doesn't support the same structures that made the Maldives famous. Constance Le Prince Maurice has some over-water suites, and Bubble Lodge on Île aux Cerfs offers an island-based glamping experience, but it's a fundamentally different product. If overwater living is the primary draw, the Maldives wins without contest.

Which has better beaches — Mauritius or Maldives?

The Maldives has a slight edge on raw beach quality — its turquoise lagoons and sandbar beaches are among the most photogenic on earth. But Mauritius offers more variety: eight distinct beach types across four coasts, from sheltered lagoons on the east coast to dramatic wind-swept bays on the south. In the Maldives, most beaches look similar regardless of resort. In Mauritius, the east coast experience is genuinely different from the west coast.

Can you visit Mauritius and the Maldives on the same trip?

Yes — both islands route naturally through Dubai, making a twin-centre honeymoon practical via Emirates. A typical itinerary: UK → Dubai → Mauritius (7–10 nights) → Dubai (1 night) → Maldives (5–7 nights) → UK. Budget from $22,000–$38,000 for two including flights, depending on resort tier at each destination.

What can you do in Mauritius that you can't do in the Maldives?

Mauritius offers land experiences the Maldives cannot: hiking Black River Gorges national park, playing championship golf (Ernie Els and Bernhard Langer courses), visiting Chamarel's seven-coloured earth, exploring Port Louis and its markets, sampling Creole and Indian cuisine at local restaurants, and renting a car to explore the whole island independently. The Maldives is essentially ocean-only — extraordinary for that, but limited beyond the water.
